About

Founded in October 2015, the NUS Buffet Response Team (BRT) is an informal group with a mission to reduce food wastage during catered events. In light of the abundance of catered events in NUS, generally there are bound to be oversupply by catering companies and under-consumption by event attendees. We believe that the unintended wastage can be avoided by directing people to the unconsumed food.

Our objective is to:

★ Provide a channel for events host to link our fellow (hungry) students and staff with unfinished buffets after events

★ Reduce food wastage during catered events

Learn More

Current Achievements

Since NUS BRT Facebook group's launch until January 2017, 11 buffet events were crowd-sourced and linked with the NUS BRT community and at least 30 trays of food were saved and diverted from our landfill. We were also featured as one of the Student Life-Hacks on Campus for NUS Student Life Fair 2016.

To improve the engagement process and reach out to more students and staff event organizers, NUS BRT has since moved to a more instantaneous platform leveraging on the Telegram mobile application. We believe that with the right use of technology, we can believe a more effective channel to link people with unfinished food. Within two months of implementation since February 2017, the group has garnered over 2,200 members and crowd-sourced 46 buffet events' food saved from going to waste!

Our Team

We are students from NUS. Started as a two-ladies team, the idea grew and team expanded to form what is now a combination of coding experts and ideators. We strive to make NUS BFT a platform to link unfinished food to those who need them more

Elaine Sam
Founder

She is always on the lookout for (food) opportunities that could help save her lunch/dinner money. The name Buffet Response Team is inspired from the group she calls herself and a bunch of friends who used to ‘respond’ to clearing food waste during her Polytechnic days. She envisions NUS BRT to be an effective mobilization
group to combat buffet waste and help hungry/needy NUS students to fill their stomach.

Gracie Low
Founder

Known by her friends as Gra, she is an active volunteer in civil society groups and is a firm believer of finishing every GRAin of rice on the plate. She is widely known as a food waste campaigner and advocates for a lifestyle without disposables.
Sparked by the fact that buffets are always not finished, she hopes NUS BFT can help link food to more people and together we can save more food from going to waste.
